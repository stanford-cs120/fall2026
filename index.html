CS120: Introduction to AI Safety

Autumn 2026

Course website under construction. Topics, readings, assignments, and policies may change before the quarter begins.

Overview

What is safe AI, and how do we make it? CS120 explores this question, focusing on the technical and socio-technical challenges of understanding, evaluating, and governing AI systems. We focus on the role of human input in the training process, unintended effects of AI development and deployment, and scalable evaluation for increasingly capable systems.

While primarily focusing on current solutions and their limitations through computer science publications, we will also discuss the labor and institutions behind AI, how evaluation is defined and audited, what oversight of AI systems could look like, and what future risks we might face. Topics will span data work, foundation models, and governance, focusing on robustness, evaluation, and auditability.

You will gain insight into why ensuring AI safety is challenging through lectures, readings, quizzes, and a final project. This course aims to prepare you to critically assess and contribute to safe AI development, equipping you with knowledge of cutting-edge research and ongoing debates in the field. The course has no official prerequisites, although familiarity with machine learning and statistics is recommended.

Working Theory

The course is organized around a theory that AI safety depends on how human inputs, data, and judgments are applied, integrated, and decided; what unintended effects arise when models amplify and recirculate them; and whether the resulting systems can be meaningfully evaluated and audited.

Coursework

The anticipated course components are weekly readings, short quizzes, participation in lecture discussions, and a final project. The final project may take the form of an empirical study, evaluation or audit, literature review, or technically grounded governance analysis. Exact grading weights, deadlines, collaboration rules, and late policies will be posted before enrollment decisions are required.
